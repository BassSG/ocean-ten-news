Lead Story
Gold Drops 4.5% This Week — Dollar Strength Overpowers Geopolitical Safe-Haven Demand
Gold entered a sharp correction this week as hawkish Fed expectations and a surging dollar overwhelmed safe-haven flows from the escalating Israel-Iran conflict. The divergence creates a critical inflection point for positioning.
What Happened
Gold fell $217 from last week's close to trade around $4,570-4,697 — the steepest weekly drop in six years. The decline came despite escalating Israel-Iran hostilities, with the yellow metal failing to benefit from its traditional safe-haven role.
So What
The dollar's surge (DXY +1.84% this month to 99.50) and the 10-year Treasury yield rising to 4.39% — the highest since July 2025 — are overpowering geopolitical tailwinds. When the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets rises faster than safe-haven demand, gold loses. The Fed's hawkish pivot is the dominant force right now.
Now What
Hold positions in $4,570-4,697 range. A break below $4,373 signals deeper correction toward $4,200. But the structural bull case remains intact: central banks buying at 77% of their projected levels, NFP weakness signaling labor market softening, and geopolitical risks that haven't peaked yet. Expect a consolidation phase before the next directional move.
